About me

Hi, I'm Vlad!
I'm currently a guest researcher at Newcastle University working on the X-MAPS project with the group of dr. Adam Ingram.
I earned my doctoral degree in the High Energy Astrophysics group at the University of Turku (UTU). In the meantime, I completed my 'non-military service' in the Computational Plasma Astrophysics group at University of Helsinki (UH). I received my Master's from Saint Petersburg State University.
At UTU, I worked under the guidance of doc. Alexandra Veledina and prof. Juri Poutanen. Our research focuses on high-energy astrophysics and polarization, with a significant portion of our work revolving around observations from the Imaging X-ray Polarimetry Explorer (IXPE).
At UH, I joined the ILLUMINATOR project under the supervision of prof. Joonas Nättilä, developing and operating the relativistic plasma simulation code runko.
My area of interest is X-ray binaries, and I study various aspects around this subject. These include computing radiative transfer and Comptonization in hot atmospheres to comprehend spectral formation; carrying out numerical simulations of magnetized astrophysical plasma turbulence to elucidate the nature of corona particle energization; and leveraging both analytical and numerical techniques for ray-tracing polarized X-rays to translate the intricate physics around compact objects into observable spectral–polarization features.
