Vladislav Loktev, graduate student

I am a fourth-year graduate student in the High Energy Astrophysics group at the University of Turku. I work under the guidance of doc. Alexandra Veledina and prof. Juri Poutanen. Our research focuses on high-energy astrophysics and polarization, with a significant portion of our current work revolving around observations from the Imaging X-ray Polarimetry Explorer (IXPE). My primary area of interest lie in the domain of X-ray binaries, and my research has encompassed various aspects of this subject. These include conducting radiative transfer and Comptonization studies in hot atmospheres to comprehend spectral formation; carrying out numerical simulations of magnetized astrophysical plasma turbulence to elucidate the nature of corona particle energization; and leveraging both analytical and numerical techniques for ray-tracing polarized X-rays to translate the intricate physics around compact objects into observable spectral-polarization features. I received my M.Sc. in Saint Petersburg State university. Please feel free to take a look at my CV.
